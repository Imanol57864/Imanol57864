## 1. They use a "Written Case" or "Presentation" Format
Unlike traditional firms where an interviewer verbally feeds you data points piece by piece, Capgemini frequently utilizes pre-read or written case studies. They provide you with financial statements, market data, or operational metrics ahead of time. Your first actual interview will not be a casual chat; it will be a high-tempo presentation or a structured deep-dive where you must pitch your findings using their data pack. [2, 3, 5, 6] 
## 2. Testing Your "Tech + Financial" Literacy Early
Capgemini focuses deeply on digital transformation and system implementation. In this domain, business cases are entirely driven by margins, cost-to-serve reductions, and ROI calculations. By giving you a data pack immediately, they are forcing you to demonstrate that you can bridge the gap between abstract business strategy and hard numbers right out of the gate. [1, 2] 
## 3. Efficiency and High Applicant Volume
As a massive global firm, Capgemini processes thousands of applications. Running purely behavioral first rounds takes up too many hours of billable consultant time. Sending a robust case preparation packet acts as a natural filter. It instantly filters out applicants who are not serious or who get overwhelmed by a profit-and-loss (P&L) statement before the first live conversation even happens. [7, 8] 
## 4. Establishing a Baseline for Later Rounds
If this is for a branch like Capgemini Invent, your interview loop will likely include both individual and group cases.
